ABOUT

The Neighbourhood are a California-based alternative rock band comprised of Jesse Rutherford, Zach Abels, Brandon Fried, Jeremy Freeman, and Mikey Margott. Known for their genre-blending sound and moody aesthetic, the band formed in 2011 and broke through in 2013 with their double-Platinum debut album I Love You. The album features the multi-platinum hit "Sweater Weather," which reached No. 1 on Billboard's Alternative chart. Following the success of their sophomore album Wiped Out! (2015) and a string of EPs, the group continued evolving their sound with The Neighbourhood (2018) and Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones (2020). With five multi-Platinum, six Platinum, and eight Gold certified singles along with two additional Platinum albums, The Neighbourhood has built a legacy of genre-defining work and are poised to begin a compelling new chapter.

I Love You

Released: 23 April 2013

The Neighbourhood's debut studio album, "I Love You," released on April 23, 2013, marked their breakthrough into the music scene. The album features a blend of alternative rock, indie pop, and R&B influences, showcasing the band's signature moody and atmospheric sound. It includes hit singles like "Sweater Weather" and "Afraid," which received widespread acclaim and commercial success. "I Love You" established The Neighbourhood as a prominent force in modern rock music, earning them a dedicated fanbase and critical praise for their innovative approach to genre fusion and lyrical depth.

#000000 & #FFFFFF

Released: 28 November 2014

"#000000 & #FFFFFF" is an EP released by The Neighbourhood on November 28, 2014. This monochromatic-themed project features a collection of tracks that delve into darker and more introspective themes, showcasing the band's versatility and depth.

Wiped Out!

Released: 30 October 2015

"Wiped Out!" is The Neighbourhood's second studio album, released on October 30, 2015. The album continues to explore the band's signature blend of alternative rock and indie pop, while also incorporating new sonic elements and experimental sounds. With tracks like "R.I.P. 2 My Youth" and "Scary Love," the album delves into themes of youth, identity, and emotional turmoil. "Wiped Out!" received positive reviews for its cohesive production and lyrical depth, solidifying The Neighbourhood's place in the contemporary music landscape.

Hard to Imagine

Released: 9 March 2018

"Hard to Imagine The Neighbourhood Ever Changing" is the third studio album by The Neighbourhood, released on March 9, 2018. This album marks a significant evolution in the band's sound, incorporating more electronic and pop influences while maintaining their moody and atmospheric style. With tracks like "Scary Love" and "Void," the album explores themes of change, self-discovery, and emotional complexity. "Hard to Imagine" received acclaim for its innovative production and introspective lyrics, further establishing The Neighbourhood's reputation as a dynamic and evolving force in alternative music.

Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones

Released: 25 September 2020

"Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones," released on September 25, 2020, is The Neighbourhood's fourth studio album. This project showcases the band's continued evolution, blending their signature alternative rock sound with more experimental and electronic elements. The album features tracks like "Sunset" and "Lost in Translation," which explore themes of identity, technology, and emotional introspection. "Chip Chrome & the Mono-Tones" received praise for its innovative approach and cohesive production, further solidifying The Neighbourhood's status as a leading force in contemporary music.
